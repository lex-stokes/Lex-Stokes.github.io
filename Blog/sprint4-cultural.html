More words! Probably hard to read words!

Understanding that the brain is a malleable tool allows people to shape their own perception of how they want their brain to function. It gives them the understanding that they can create and train the skills they want to. If people understand that they can change the entire way that their brain functions, and how, then they are more likely to apply the principles of neuroplasticity to their lives. They are going to engage in activities that they may find harder or more taxing with the concept that the more they do it the easier it will become.

If one regularly does a crossword for example one will get better at crosswords, even if those few you google the answers you’ll remember it and slowly gain a better understanding of crosswords. Over time less and less googling will occur. I do this with puzzles as well and often have memory games on my games list to try and improve in areas I feel lacking in.

There are so many ways to increase your neuroplasticity. It’s very much about finding what works best for you and when, perhaps something you are doing now isn’t clicking and you just need to stretch your brain in a different way! Go read some fiction, learn or practice an instrument, or for a more tricky activity non dominant hand exercises but most importantly SLEEP!!!

“If you imagine less, less will be what you undoubtedly deserve”

Growth mindset is the idea that one is capable of changing and growing. One with a growth mindset is more focussed on learning and sees failure as an opportunity to learn. They aren’t confined to the idea of wanting to make sure they succeed in order to seem smart, which allows for them to repeat tasks with the knowledge that they will improve. It’s a mindset best suited for learning almost any task, and developing a skill.

“Personal success is when you work your hardest to become your best,”

A lot of study results, many of the medical ones are relatively new when focussing on learning, especially regarding adults. But the statistics are really overwhelming around the positive nature, and that the fixed mindset is more likely to cause people to lie about their results.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

I already have! I’ve set myself time goals and reminded myself to do a little bit every day. While not always achieving this I can definitely see the days when I haven’t been coding. If I am struggling with something then I give myself some time to ponder, if I get frustrated then I take a short break and come back to it. After that, if all my googling has been fruitless, I ask for help! I constantly remind myself that while it’s over my head today if I look back to the first week some of that was over my head too and now I can do it, most of the time, pretty well. And in the future it’ll be easier.
